What is an ATS Resume Checker?

An ATS resume checker is a tool that analyzes your resume to ensure it's compatible with Applicant Tracking Systems. These systems are used by over 98% of Fortune 500 companies to filter job applications.

A good ATS resume scanner will check for:

  • Keyword optimization - Does your resume contain the right keywords from the job description?
  • Format compatibility - Can the ATS properly read and parse your resume?
  • Section structure - Are your resume sections properly labeled?
  • ATS score - How well does your resume match the job requirements?

Understanding Your ATS Resume Score

Your ATS resume score indicates how well your resume matches a specific job posting. Here's what different scores mean:

  • 90-100%: Excellent match - Your resume is highly optimized for this job
  • 80-89%: Good match - Strong chance of passing ATS filters
  • 70-79%: Fair match - Consider adding more relevant keywords
  • Below 70%: Needs improvement - Your resume may be filtered out

Tips for Better ATS Resume Score

1. Use Keywords from the Job Description

ATS systems scan for specific keywords. Include job-relevant terms throughout your resume. KUPOSU AI does this automatically by analyzing each job posting.

2. Keep Formatting Simple

Avoid tables, columns, images, and fancy graphics. Use a clean, single-column layout that ATS can easily parse.

3. Use Standard Section Headings

Stick to conventional headings like "Work Experience," "Education," and "Skills." Creative headings may confuse ATS systems.

4. Include Both Acronyms and Full Terms

Write "Search Engine Optimization (SEO)" instead of just "SEO" to ensure ATS catches both versions.

5. Tailor Your Resume for Each Job

Generic resumes get low ATS scores. Customize your resume for each application - or let KUPOSU AI do it for you automatically.

6. Submit in the Right Format

Most ATS systems work best with .docx or .pdf files. KUPOSU AI generates perfectly formatted PDFs that are guaranteed to be ATS-compatible.
