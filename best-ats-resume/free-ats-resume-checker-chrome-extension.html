What is an ATS Resume Checker Chrome Extension?

An ATS resume checker chrome extension is a browser add-on that analyzes your resume directly while you browse job sites. Instead of switching between tabs or uploading files to websites, these extensions work right where you need them—on LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, and other job platforms.

Key features of ATS checker extensions include:

  • Keyword Analysis: Scans job descriptions and compares with your resume
  • ATS Score: Gives you a compatibility score (usually 0-100)
  • Missing Keywords: Shows which important terms you're missing
  • Formatting Check: Identifies ATS-unfriendly formatting
  • One-Click Access: Works directly on job sites

  3. Browse Job Listings: Go to LinkedIn, Indeed, or any job site
  4. Click the Extension: When you find a job, click the extension icon
  5. Review Your Score: See how well your resume matches
  6. Optimize: Use suggestions to improve (or let KUPOSU AI do it automatically)

What to Look for in a Free ATS Checker Extension

1. Accurate Keyword Matching

The extension should identify both hard skills (Python, Excel) and soft skills (leadership, communication) from job descriptions.

2. Real ATS Score

Look for extensions that provide a numerical score based on actual ATS algorithms, not just generic tips.

3. Actionable Feedback

Good extensions tell you exactly what's missing and how to fix it—not just that your resume "needs improvement."

4. Privacy Protection

Your resume contains personal information. Choose extensions that process data locally or have clear privacy policies.

5. Works on Multiple Sites

The best extensions work across LinkedIn, Indeed, Glassdoor, and company career pages.

Common ATS Issues These Extensions Catch

  • Missing Keywords: Important skills mentioned in job but not in your resume
  • Wrong File Format: Using .pdf when .docx is better (or vice versa)
  • Tables and Graphics: Many ATS can't read these
  • Fancy Fonts: Stick to Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman
  • Headers/Footers: ATS often skips these sections
  • Abbreviations: Using "ML" when job says "Machine Learning"

Pro Tip: Always check your ATS score before applying. A score above 70% significantly increases your chances of getting past automated filters.
